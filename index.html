The largest city in France and its capital, Paris is one of the most prestigious global centers for art, architecture, fashion and cuisine. It attracts millions of tourists each year from all over the globe and is well known for its iconic structures and world class museums - not to mention the delicious food. Whatever your interests may be, there is always something for you to enjoy in this diverse and multideminsonal city; with this guide, you won't miss out on any of the city's biggest attractions and can be guaranteed a magical experience in the City of Lights.

Bienvenue à Paris!

Basic French Phrases to Know

Before diving into the details of your trip, it will probably be helpful to know some basic French terms, as French is the dominant language in Paris. Provided below are some essential phrases that will help you navigate your stay and communicate with native French inhabitants.

French Phrase English Meaning
Bonjour Hello
S'il vous plaît Please
Oui/Non Yes/No
Merci Thank you
Excuse-moi Excuse me
Pardon Sorry
Où est... ? Where is... ?
C'est combien ? How much is it?
Parles-tu anglais ? Do you speak English?

Places to Go

Paris offers several fascinating attractions that lure travelers from all around the world and from all aspects of life. Whether it be art, architecture, or just a beautiful view, the city never fails to leave visitors in awe and never wanting to leave! Listed below are some of the top attractions that you should definitely not miss.

  • The Eiffel Tower

    As a signature monument of Paris, the Eiffel Tower is ranked to be one of the most visited attractions in the city. Its stunning architectural design is a key feature of the Parisian skyline and makes for a stunning photo opportunity! Visitors can walk up or take an elevator ride to the first and second levels of the tower, from which they are greeted with a beautiful view of the city down below. Additionally, every night for about five minutes, the tower lights up to display extraordinary illuminations on the city - something to definitely look out for during your trip!

    Source: Jess Mchugh, 17 Gorgeous Photos of the Eiffel Tower at Night, 2018, travelandleisure.com, https://imagesvc.meredithcorp.io/v3/mm/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fstatic.onecms.io%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2Fsites%2F28%2F2018%2F01%2Ftrocadero-eiffel-tower-paris-EIFFELPM0118.jpg

  • The Arc de Triomphe and the Champs-Élysées

    The Champs-Élysées, a world famous Parisian avenue spanning nearly 2 kilometers in length, is another beautiful sight that can be enjoyed from the City of Lights. Each day, it is visited by approximately 300,000 people and was even dubbed the "La Plus Belle Avenue du Monde", or the most beautiful avenue in the world. It is dotted with several shops, restaurants and stores, and by driving down the avenue, one can also get a stunning head-on view of the Arc de Triomphe.

    Source: Lorraine Murray, Arc de Triomphe, briticanna.com, https://cdn.britannica.com/s:700x500/37/123937-050-0B92318A/Place-Arc-de-Triomphe-Paris-Charles-Gaulle.jpg, https://cdn.britannica.com/s:700x500/37/123937-050-0B92318A/Place-Arc-de-Triomphe-Paris-Charles-Gaulle.jpg

  • The Louvre

    As the world's largest art museum built in 1783 with nearly 380,000 pieces of art and spanning more than half a million square feet in area, the Louvre is a historic monument in Paris and a central landmark of the city. Ranging from Ancient Egyptian mummies to the iconic Mona Lisa, the Louvre offers a wide variety of art and is filled with fascinating historical significance. Since it would be nearly impossible to view the whole museum in one day, it is a good idea to plan out or make a list of all the works you want to see beforehand!

    Source: Jessie Szalay, The Louvre Museum: Facts, Paintings & Tickets, 2018, livescience.com https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/EGUiTQh5BLN6ZVHcFUC2fS.jpg

Bon Appétite!

Calling all foodies! In addition to being a global center for art, architecture and fashion, Paris also offers many delicous food options, as cuisine is an integral aspect of Parisian culture and has played a key role in bringing the French people together. Listed below are top foods that you should be sure not to miss out on during your trip.

  • Crepes: A crepe is a very thin pancake that is wrapped around a filling, such as chocolate sauce, fresh fruit, or cream. As a popular French food, you can try crepes at cafes or even from small vendors on the streets.

    Source: Crêpe Canaveral; 2015, nytimes.com, https://static01.nyt.com/images/2015/05/27/dining/20150527HUNGRY-slide-MBNS/20150527HUNGRY-slide-MBNS-superJumbo.jpg?quality=75&auto=webp&disable=upscale

  • Macarons: Macarons are a classic French dessert, consisting of two chewy almond cookies that are sandwiched around a flavorful filling. They come in a wide range of flavors, including hazelnut, raspberry, or pistachio, and can be enjoyed at local macaron cafes. Listed below are some of the best pastry and chocolate houses in Paris.

    • Pierre Hermé
    • Ladurée (my personal favorite!)
    • Carette

    Source: Lindsey Tramuta, Paris's Best Macarons: A Guide, 2017, nytimes.com, https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/07/21/t-magazine/food/macarons-slide-ORK0/macarons-slide-ORK0-superJumbo.jpg?quality=90&auto=webp

  • Escargot: Escargot, a beloved traditional French delicacy, is a dish of edible land snails. It is offered at many restaurants as an appetizer and is commonly paired with herb-infused butters, such as garlic, thyme or parsley.
